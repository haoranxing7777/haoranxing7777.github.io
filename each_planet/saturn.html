Saturn (6th)

The Remarkable Thinness of Saturn's Rings

They are incredibly thin compared to their width! Although they span up to 282,000 kilometers (about 175,000 miles) from the planet, the rings are only about 10 meters (33 feet) thick on average. This means that if you could stack the entire thickness of the rings, it would be less than the height of a tall building! The rings are primarily made up of ice particles, with some rocky debris, and their stunning appearance makes Saturn one of the most recognizable planets in the solar system.
