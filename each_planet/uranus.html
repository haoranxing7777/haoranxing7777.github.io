Uranus: The Planet That Rolls on Its Side

Unlike most planets in the solar system, which have an upright rotation axis, Uranus is tilted at an angle of about 98 degrees. This extreme tilt means that its poles are nearly in the plane of its orbit around the Sun. As a result, Uranus experiences unique seasons, with each pole getting 42 years of continuous sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness! This unusual orientation contributes to its distinct and complex weather patterns.
