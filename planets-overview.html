Solar System Overview

Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of the solar system. It provides the necessary heat and light for life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has a very thin atmosphere.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for its thick, toxic atmosphere. It is the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only planet known to support life. It has a diverse environment.

Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance from iron oxide on its surface.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its Great Red Spot and many moons.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, made mostly of ice particles, and is the second-largest planet.

Uranus

Uranus is known for its unique blue color and sideways rotation. It has a set of faint rings.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun, known for its strong winds and deep blue color.

Pluto

Pluto is classified as a dwarf planet. It has a complex orbit and is known for its icy surface.
